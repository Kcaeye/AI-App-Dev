GUIDE
Science & Strategy
Principle 01
Progressive Overload
Your muscles grow when consistently challenged. Gradually increase resistance, reps, or intensity over time. Small weekly increases compound into extraordinary results.
Principle 02
Protein Is Non-Negotiable
Aim for 0.7–1g of protein per pound of body weight daily. Protein provides the amino acids needed to repair and build muscle tissue after every session.
Principle 03
Lifting Won't Bulk You Up
Women have roughly 10–20x less testosterone than men — the primary hormone driving muscle size. Resistance training sculpts, strengthens, and boosts metabolism. The "bulk" myth is fitness's most persistent lie.
Principle 04
Sleep Is Where Growth Happens
Muscles don't grow during your workout — they grow during recovery. Human growth hormone peaks during deep sleep. Prioritize 7–9 hours nightly. Overtraining with poor sleep is a recipe for stagnation.
Principle 05
Fuel Your Training
Undereating is one of the most common barriers to muscle building for women. Your body needs sufficient calories to repair muscle tissue and fuel workouts. A moderate surplus supports lean muscle gain.
Principle 06
Bone Density & Longevity
Resistance training is one of the most effective ways to increase bone density and combat age-related muscle loss. Starting a strength program at any age pays dividends for decades.
